# Alpaka
## Jurnal laporan 
The alpaca (Vicugna pacos) is a domesticated mammal native to South America that is now widely conserved ex situ in conservation institutions such as zoos. This study aims to identify the daily behaviors of adult and newborn alpacas at Gembira Loka Zoo in Yogyakarta, as well as to evaluate their welfare levels based on the Five Freedoms principles. Observations were conducted over 5 days in January 2026 using the ad libitum sampling method with continuous recording via CCTV and direct observation. Data were analyzed descriptively by calculating the relative frequency and duration of behaviors. The results showed that daily behaviors in both mothers and infants were dominated by locomotor activities, with frequencies of 24% and 42%, respectively. The longest duration for both subjects was found in resting behavior. Although the frequency of feeding behavior was lower compared to the literature in open habitats, overall, the alpacas’ welfare aspects were well met, as indicated by normal growth, the absence of abnormal behavior, and the availability of supportive facilities in the enclosure.
